The purpose of a web browser is to take the information given to it by a web page, and to render it for you to view. Due to how different companies and browsers want content to be viewed, some browsers can render things differently to others. At best, this could mean some minor graphical glitches or none at all. At worst, this could cause a loss of functionality. Upon requesting access for a website, the browser can see the HTML, CSS, and any other relevant files for the page. The browser then takes those documents, and follows their instructions to build what you see on the screen.
